Nathia Gali

Places to Visit:

  • Nathiagali Church: This historic church, built during the British colonial era, is a serene and picturesque place to visit. It offers a sense of tranquility and stunning architecture..
  • Nathiagali-Ayubia Chairlift: Enjoy a thrilling chairlift ride that connects Nathiagali with Ayubia. It provides an excellent vantage point to enjoy the surrounding natural beauty..
  • Dunga Gali Pine Line Track: This walking trail is surrounded by tall pine trees and is an excellent place for a leisurely stroll. It's especially beautiful during the summer and autumn seasons..
  • Restaurants and Cafes: Nathiagali has a few eateries that offer local and traditional Pakistani cuisine. Don't miss the opportunity to savor some local dishes.
  • Thandiani: Although a bit farther away, Thandiani is another hill station known for its cool climate and serene surroundings. It's a great place for a day trip from Nathiagali.

Things to Do:

  • Hiking: Explore the beautiful hiking trails in the region.
  • Photography: The scenic beauty of Nathiagali provides ample opportunities for photography. Capture the stunning landscapes, colorful flora, and charming architecture..
  • Horse Riding: You can go horseback riding through the meadows and hills, offering a unique way to explore the area.
  • Wildlife Spotting: Keep an eye out for local wildlife, such as monkeys and various bird species, as you explore the forests and parks in the area.

Local Tips:

  • Pack Warm Clothing: Even in the summer, Nathiagali can get chilly, especially at night. Pack warm clothing, including sweaters, jackets, and extra layers.
  • Travel Insurance: Consider purchasing travel insurance that covers unexpected events, such as trip cancellations or medical emergencies.
  • Safety Precautions: Follow safety guidelines when trekking or hiking, including informing someone about your plans and carrying necessary safety equipment.
  • Check Weather and Road Conditions: Before heading to Nathiagali, check the weather conditions and road accessibility. The region can experience heavy snowfall in the winter, which may impact travel plans.

    Naran Kaghan

    Places to Visit:

    • Lulusar Lake: This is the largest lake in the Kaghan Valley and is known for its serene beauty. It's a popular spot for picnics and photography.
    • Babusar Pass: A high mountain pass that connects Naran to Chilas and the Karakoram Highway. The views from this pass are incredible, but it is usually accessible only in the summer months due to heavy snowfall.
    • Saif-ul-Mulook Lake: This is one of the most famous and picturesque lakes in the region, known for its clear blue waters and surrounding snow-capped peaks. Boating and trekking are popular activities here.
    • Lalazar: A high-altitude plateau located about 21 kilometers from Naran, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. It's a great place for a day trip.
    • Shogran: A lovely hill station with pine forests and panoramic views of the Kaghan Valley. You can take a jeep ride to Siri and Paye Meadows from here.

      • Things to Do:

      • Boating: Rent a paddleboat or rowboat to explore the serene Saif-ul-Mulook Lake. It's a great way to enjoy the crystal-clear waters and surrounding mountain views.
      • Camping: Camping is a fantastic way to immerse yourself in nature. There are various campsites available, and you can either bring your own camping gear or rent it locally.
      • Jeep Safaris: Take a thrilling jeep safari to visit remote areas like Babusar Pass and surrounding high-altitude regions that may not be accessible by regular vehicles.
      • White Water Rafting: Depending on the season and water levels, you might be able to enjoy white water rafting in the Kunhar River.

      Local Tips:

      • Responsible Camping:If camping, obtain the necessary permits and camp only in designated areas. Make sure your campfire is safe, and fully extinguish it before leaving.
      • Enjoy the Silence:Naran and Kaghan are known for their natural beauty and tranquility. Take time to appreciate the peace and quiet of the area.
